1 12/03/2023 Dr , I want to know about pharmacokinetic? Pharmacokinetic is a study of drug movement. It involves the following Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion.
2 14/04/2023 Dr, What happen when drug enters our blood stream. Please clarify ? Once in the bloodstream, the drug is distributed throughout the body. The distribution is influenced by blood flow, the drug's affinity for tissue, and the permeability of cell membranes. The volume of distribution is a key parameter that describes the extent to which a drug is distributed in the body's tissues.
3 16/04/2023 Dr I don't understand volume of distribution . Is it the same at each parts of the body. Volume of distribution (VOT): theoretical volume that represents the degree to which a drug is distributed in the body tissues and in the plasma. It is the same throughout the body.
4 21/03/2023 Dr , is it true Large VOT is for fat people . Actually large VOT indicates greater volume than total body water. Technically it is correct.
5 24/03/2023 Dr , why there are two stages in metabolism of drug . please detail it Metabolism has two main phases. Functionalise reactions (Phase 1) and (Phase 2) Conjugation reactions. Drug metabolism converts lipophilic chemical compounds into more readily excreted hydrophilic products. Involves TWO phases of metabolism, This process generally increases the water solubility of the drug, facilitating its excretion in urine or bile. Phase II reactions typically result in detoxification but, in some cases, can also produce active or even toxic metabolites
6 5/3/2023 Dr can you briefly explain the toxicology? Toxicology is a study of the adverse effects that occur in living organisms due to chemicals. It involves observing and reporting symptoms, mechanisms, detection, and treatments of toxic substances, particularly the poisoning of humans.
7 8/5/2023 Dr, I don't understand the drug-response relationship It is about dose of a substance and its effect in a biological system, which is crucial for understanding drug efficacy, safety, and toxicity risk.
8 9/5/2023 How about receptors Dr ?All drugs must bind to its receptors to produce its effects? So enzymes are also receptors ? Receptors are responsible for selectivity of drug action. The molecular size, shape, and electrical charge of a drug determine whether—and with what affinity—it will bind to a particular receptor among the vast array of chemically different binding sites available in a cell, tissue, or patient. Yes, enzymes also one of the receptors. Receptors are typically proteins including, ion channels, or G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), that play critical roles in cellular function.
9 15/05/2023 Dr Aliana, is specificity and Affinity has similar definition in Drug-Receptor Interactions No, Specificity means the drug is designed to bind to specific receptors based on the shape, charge, and chemical properties of the drug molecule. This specificity determines the selectivity of the drug's action. Affinity refers to the strength of the drug-receptor binding. Drugs with high affinity for their receptors will bind more tightly and usually require lower doses to achieve a therapeutic effect. Affinity is influenced by the chemical structure of the drug and the receptor.
10 16/05/2023 Efficacy of drug means drug binds to receptor and elicit response. Is it the same with potency? No, Efficacy is distinct from potency, which is a measure of the drug's effectiveness in terms of its concentration or dose.
11 16/05/2023 Dr why inverse agonist has opposite effects than agonist? Inverse agonists bind to the same receptor as agonists but induce a pharmacological response opposite to that of agonists. They stabilize the receptor in its inactive conformation, reducing its activity below the basal level. They are particularly relevant for receptors that exhibit constitutive activity, meaning the receptor is active even in the absence of a ligand.
12 17/03/2025 How partial agonist work Dr As the name implies, partial agonists bind to and activates a receptor but produces a weaker, or partial, response compared to a full agonist. Partial agonists have intermediate efficacy; they can activate receptor but not to the full extent that a full agonist can. In the presence of a full agonist, partial agonists can act as competitive antagonists because they can compete for the same receptor sites and reduce the overall effect by occupying receptors without producing a maximal response.
